What is a Game? - Defined by amusement fails -> what about a Chess Grandmaster? - Defined by competition fails -> what about Solitaire? - Defined by rules fails (but not by much) -> what about Mao? (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao_(card_game))
1930s - Ludwig Wittgenstein - One of the first academic philosophers that addressed the definition of the word game - Lots of interesting facts - Born in Vienna into one of Europe's richest families (2nd only to the Rothschilds) - Wittgenstein and Hitler were born just six days apart and went to the same school for a time - Served as an officer on the front line during WW1, and sectretly as an orderly in WW2 - Argued that we don't have a true definition of the word game, and we don't need one - Elements such as play, rules, and competition fail to adequately define what a game is
